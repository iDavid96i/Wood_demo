25+ Years of Craft

The Artist

A lifetime spent listening to the grain

Every piece begins as a quiet conversation with the wood. For over two decades, our craftsman has shaped raw hardwood into objects meant to be held, gifted, and passed down — guided by patience, an old set of chisels, and a deep respect for the material.

The work is unhurried and entirely by hand. From the first carved line to the final coat of natural oil, nothing is mass-produced. Each hamsa, box, and decorative piece carries the marks of the person who made it — and the story of the person it's made for.

  • ✦ Traditional hand-carving techniques
  • ✦ Natural oils & finishes only
  • ✦ Personalized, made-to-order work

Materials

The Woods We Work

Each species brings its own colour, scent and character to the finished piece.

Cherry

Warm reddish tones that deepen beautifully with age and light. A favourite for heirloom pieces.

African Walnut

Rich golden-brown with a lustrous ribbon grain — dramatic, refined and full of depth.

American Walnut

Classic chocolate hues prized for fine furniture and detailed carving alike.

Mahogany

Deep, even reddish-brown with a timeless elegance and remarkable workability.

Wenge

Almost-black, dramatic and bold — a striking contrast for modern, sculptural work.

Beech

Pale, fine and dependable. A clean canvas that takes detail and finish exceptionally well.
